    Coming soon: Flying Simulator 2009

    We are currently in a pre-alpha stage. For now check out the airport search through the tab "Airports of the world" above.

    Get on board and fly the modern passenger planes of the world. This is by far the most realistic flight simulator ever made, with events happening real-time according to detailed statistical analysis.

    • Fly between almost all airports in the world, over 3900 will be available in the first version(data delivered by ICAO)
    • Slick flying interface made in Adobe Flash, with unprecedented graphics and realism
    • Tight integration with Google maps for keeping track of your itinerary and previous flights
    • Earn frequent flier miles for your flights and compete with other people online
    • Actual weather from around the world integrated into simulation provides ground breaking experience
    • Gather flags from around the world as you visit the most remote airports on earth. Can you collect them all?
    • All aspects of flying are covered, you would swear that you were on the inside of a plane
    • The best alternative to the recently cancelled Microsoft Flight Simulator

    About flyingsim.com

    We aim to please

    Please note that although we aim to give the experience of a life time, we do not in any way refund time wasted on this site.

    Data

    flyingsim.com uses ICAO DAFIF data from the excellent http://data.x-plane.com/ under GPL license
    Please do not "mine" this site, instead get the data from code.google.com

    We also have a collaboration under way with the people behind the growing site http://openflights.org/.
    OpenFlights is a site that lets you map your flights around the world, search and filter them in all sorts of interesting ways,
    calculate statistics automatically, and share your flights and trips with friends and the entire world (if you wish).
    Check out their FAQ to see if this site is for you.

    Technology

    This site runs on Google AppEngine using the Django framework for Python. The front-end is a mash-up of flash built with Adobe Flex and Google Maps.

    We of course strive to have our solution cover as many buzz words and different technologies as possible. The current list includes:

    • Mashup
